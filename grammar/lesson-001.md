# Grammar — Lesson 1: Verbs Don't Conjugate by Person

Unlike English ("I am, you are, he is"), Danish verbs use **one form for every person** in the present tense.

Verb: **at hedde** ("to be called")

| Pronoun | Danish | English |
|---|---|---|
| Jeg (I) | jeg hedder | I am called |
| Du (you) | du hedder | you are called |
| Han/Hun (he/she) | han/hun hedder | he/she is called |

Sentence pattern: `Jeg hedder [navn].` → "My name is [name]." (literally "I am called [name]")

This "no conjugation by person" rule applies to **all** Danish verbs in the present tense — a big simplification compared to English.
