# Pre-Departure Routine (15–20 Days)

**Start date:** 2026-07-14 (day after Lesson 1)
**Goal:** By the end of this window, be solidly through Module 1 (Survival Basics) and Module 2 (Grammar Core) from `curriculum.md`, and into the start of Module 3 (Shopping & Café) — Lessons 002 through ~010. That's enough to introduce yourself, describe things, form correct present-tense sentences, and handle basic shopping/ordering by the time you land in Denmark.

This plan is built for **18 days** (2026-07-14 → 2026-07-31). If you only have 15, drop the two dedicated `/review` days (Day 6 and Day 14) and go straight through — you'll still land around Lesson 009–010. If you have the full 20, use the extra 2 days as no-guilt buffer/catch-up days wherever life gets in the way (packing, visa paperwork, travel prep).

## Daily Habit (every single day, ~15–20 min, even on lesson days)

Do this regardless of what else is scheduled:
- **5 min** — Anki or Memrise review of vocabulary from *all* lessons so far (not just the latest one)
- **10 min** — Duolingo Danish (keeps the alphabet/pronunciation reflexes warm)
- **5 min** — Pick 3 words from any `vocabulary/lesson-XXX.md` file and listen to them on Forvo until you can mimic the sound

This daily habit matters more than any single lesson — consistency beats intensity for pronunciation and retention.

## If a day gets skipped

Don't chain-skip. If you miss a homework day, just do the daily habit and push the next lesson back by one day — the sequence matters more than hitting exact dates. Do **not** skip straight to the next lesson without submitting homework; per `CLAUDE.md`, corrections on your actual writing are how mistakes get caught before they calcify.

## After Day 18

You'll be about a quarter of the way through Module 3. Once you've landed and settled (CPR number, housing sorted), the priority shifts to enrolling in official `danskuddannelse` classes (see `curriculum.md` → Resources) — those will absorb most of the grammar heavy-lifting going forward, and this lesson series can shift toward reinforcement, DTU-specific vocabulary, and speaking confidence.
