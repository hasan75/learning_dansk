# Lesson 1: Hello, Denmark! (Alphabet, Pronunciation & Greetings)

**CEFR Level:** A0 → A1 (starting point)
**Date:** 2026-07-13

## 1. Lesson Goal

- Learn the Danish alphabet and how it differs from English
- Learn to greet people and introduce yourself
- Understand the first Danish grammar rule: verbs don't conjugate by person

## 3. Pronunciation

### The Danish Alphabet
29 letters: the 26 English letters, plus **Æ, Ø, Å** at the end.

- **æ** — like "a" in English "cat"
- **ø** — like French "eu" / German "ö"
- **å** — like "aw" in English "law" (older spelling: "aa", e.g. *Aalborg*)

### Key sounds
- Danish **"d"** after a vowel is often soft, close to English "th" in "this" (e.g. in *hedder*).
- Final **"g"** is often silent or very soft — not a hard English "g"/"k" (e.g. *Tak*).
- **Stød** (marked ˀ in IPA): a glottal-stop "catch" in the voice, like the pause in "uh-oh". A signature feature of Danish; more on this in later lessons.
- Common mistake: over-pronouncing every letter. Danish speech "swallows" syllables — don't panic if it sounds mumbled at first.

## 4. Grammar: Danish Verbs Don't Conjugate by Person

Unlike English ("I am, you are, he is"), Danish verbs use **one form for every person**.

Verb: **at hedde** ("to be called")

| Pronoun | Danish | English |
|---|---|---|
| Jeg (I) | jeg hedder | I am called |
| Du (you) | du hedder | you are called |
| Han/Hun (he/she) | han/hun hedder | he/she is called |

Sentence pattern: `Jeg hedder [navn].` → "My name is [name]."

## 5. Reading Practice

> Hej! Jeg hedder Peter. Velkommen! Farvel, vi ses. Tak!

## 6. Listening Practice

> **A:** **Hej!** Jeg **hed**der Anna.
> **B:** God**dag**, Anna! Jeg **hed**der Lars.
> **A:** Vel**kom**men, Lars!
> **B:** **Tak**! Vi **ses**.
> **A:** **Far**vel!

Read aloud, lightly leaning on the bold stressed syllables. Danish rhythm is flatter/more even than English.

## 9. Exercises

**A. Fill in the blank**
1. Jeg _______ Rahim. (am called) → *hedder*
2. _______! Velkommen til Danmark. (Hi) → *Hej*

**B. Matching**
1. Farvel → b. Goodbye
2. Tak → a. Thanks
3. Undskyld → c. Excuse me

**C. Multiple choice** — Which letter is NOT in the English alphabet? → a) Å

**D. Translation** — "Good evening, my name is Rahim." → *Godaften, jeg hedder Rahim.*
