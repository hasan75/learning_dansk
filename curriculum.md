# Danish Curriculum — A0 to B2

This is the master roadmap for all lessons. `/new-lesson` reads this file (alongside `progress.md`) to decide what to teach next. Grammar is sequenced so nothing is used before it's taught; the four practical threads from `CLAUDE.md` (Daily Life, DTU, Part-Time Jobs, Professional/Technical Danish) are woven in once enough grammar exists to support them.

**How to read the tables:** `Status` is updated after each lesson (☐ pending → ✅ done). This must stay in sync with the `Lesson Log` table in `progress.md` — if they ever disagree, `progress.md` is the source of truth for what's actually been taught, and this file should be corrected to match.

**Pre-departure routine:** see `routine.md` for a day-by-day 15–20 day schedule that walks through Lessons 002–010 (Modules 1–2 and the start of Module 3) before arriving in Denmark.

## Resources

### Structured / Official Courses (do this — it's free)
Denmark provides **free Danish classes** (*danskuddannelse*) to residents with a CPR number, which you'll get as a DTU student. This is by far the most effective resource — real teachers, structured levels (Danskuddannelse 1–3), and official exams (PD1, PD2, PD3, Studieprøven) recognized for residency/work purposes. Once you have your CPR number, sign up through your municipality (Kommune) — in the Copenhagen area, common providers include:
- **Studieskolen** (Copenhagen)
- **IA Sprog** (Copenhagen)
- **Sprogcenter** options vary by municipality — check your Kommune's website after moving
- **DTU International Affairs / DTU Learn** — check for Danish courses offered directly to international students when you arrive

### Apps & Self-Study
- **Duolingo** — free, gamified daily practice (Danish course available)
- **Memrise** — spaced-repetition vocabulary decks for Danish
- **Anki** — free flashcard app; you can build custom decks from your `vocabulary/lesson-XXX.md` files
- **Clozemaster** — vocabulary in full-sentence context, good once you're past A1

### Dictionaries & Pronunciation
- **Den Danske Ordbog** (ordnet.dk) — the official Danish dictionary, includes audio pronunciation for nearly every word
- **Forvo** (forvo.com) — crowdsourced native pronunciation recordings, useful for tricky words

### Listening & Reading
- **DR Ligetil** (dr.dk) — Danish news written in simplified/easy Danish; great from A2 onward
- **DR** (Danmarks Radio) — mainstream news/radio/TV for B1+ listening practice, many programs have Danish subtitles
- Danish children's/picture books from your local library — excellent low-pressure reading practice for A1–A2

### Conversation Practice
- **italki** or **Preply** — book affordable native Danish tutors for speaking practice
- **Tandem** / **HelloTalk** — free language exchange apps; trade English help for Danish practice with locals
- **r/Danish** and **r/Denmark** (Reddit) — informal Q&A and culture insight

### Practical / Government
- **Life in Denmark** (official government portal, lifeindenmark.borger.dk) — CPR, MitID, taxes, residence — pairs well with Lesson 026 (Government Offices)
- **Copenhagen International House** / **SIRI** — support services for internationals, often connected to danskuddannelse enrollment

*Note: always double-check current URLs/enrollment steps yourself when the time comes — official program details and providers can change.*
